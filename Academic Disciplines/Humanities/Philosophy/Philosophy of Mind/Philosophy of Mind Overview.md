# Philosophy of Mind Overview

Philosophy of mind examines fundamental questions about consciousness, mental phenomena, the relationship between mind and body, and the nature of thoughts, experiences, and subjective awareness.

## Core Problems and Questions

- The "hard problem" of consciousness
- The mind-body problem
- What is consciousness?
- What is the relationship between mental events and physical events?
- Do we have free will?
- What is personal identity?

## Major Philosophical Positions

### Metaphysical Approaches
- Dualism (Cartesian and substance dualism)
- Physicalism and materialism
- Property dualism and non-reductive physicalism
- Functionalism and computationalism
- Panpsychism

### Theories of Consciousness
- Higher-order thought theories
- Global workspace theory
- Integrated information theory
- Predictive processing
- Enactivism

### Intentionality and Content
- Mental content and representation
- The "frame problem"
- Externalism about mental content
- Internalism about mental states
