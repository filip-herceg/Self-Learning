# Sports Psychology Overview

Sports psychology applies psychological principles to athletes, teams, and sports organizations, examining performance enhancement, injury prevention, and psychological well-being in athletic contexts.

## Performance Enhancement

### Mental Skills Training
- Goal setting and planning
- Visualization and mental imagery
- Self-talk and positive thinking
- Attention and concentration
- Arousal regulation and relaxation
- Confidence and self-efficacy

### Coaching Psychology
- Coach-athlete communication
- Leadership and motivation
- Team cohesion and dynamics
- Conflict resolution
- Stress management in coaching

### Competitive Anxiety and Pressure
- Performance anxiety
- Choking under pressure
- Flow states and optimal performance
- Pre-competition routines

## Injury and Rehabilitation

### Psychological Responses to Injury
- Trauma responses to athletic injury
- Adjustment and coping
- Pain perception and management
- Return-to-play decisions

### Rehabilitation Psychology
- Adherence to rehabilitation
- Mental skills during recovery
- Psychological readiness to return
- Career transitions and retirement

## Team and Organizational Psychology

### Team Dynamics
- Group cohesion and performance
- Collective efficacy
- Team communication
- Conflict in teams

### Organizational Aspects
- Sport psychology consulting
- Ethical issues in sport psychology
- Prevention of abuse and harassment

## Special Populations

- Youth athletes
- Female athletes
- Athletes with disabilities
- Elite and professional athletes

## Mental Health in Sports

- Depression and anxiety in athletes
- Eating disorders and body image
- Substance use
- Suicide prevention
