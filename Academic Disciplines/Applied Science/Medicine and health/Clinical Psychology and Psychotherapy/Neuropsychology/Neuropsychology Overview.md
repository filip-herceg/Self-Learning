# Neuropsychology Overview

Neuropsychology examines the relationships between brain function and behavior, assessment of cognitive and behavioral deficits following brain injury or disease, and rehabilitation interventions.

## Topics

### Brain-Behavior Relationships
- Localization of function
- Hemispheric specialization
- Neural plasticity and recovery
- Distributed networks and behavior

### Assessment and Diagnosis
- Neuropsychological batteries and testing
- Cognitive screening instruments
- Behavioral and emotional assessment
- Differential diagnosis

### Acquired Brain Injury
- Traumatic brain injury (TBI)
- Stroke and cerebrovascular accident
- Anoxic brain injury
- Closed-head injury

### Neurodegenerative Diseases
- Alzheimer's disease and dementia
- Parkinson's disease
- Frontotemporal dementia
- Huntington's disease
- ALS and other motor neuron diseases

### Developmental Disorders
- ADHD and executive function
- Learning disabilities
- Autism spectrum disorder
- Cerebral palsy

### Rehabilitation and Recovery
- Cognitive rehabilitation techniques
- Behavioral modification approaches
- Compensatory strategies
- Neuroplasticity-based interventions
- Family support and adaptation
