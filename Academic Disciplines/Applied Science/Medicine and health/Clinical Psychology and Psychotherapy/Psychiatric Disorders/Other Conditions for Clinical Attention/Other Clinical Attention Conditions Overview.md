# Other Conditions for Clinical Attention Overview

This section captures conditions and circumstances that may be the focus of clinical attention (DSM-5 V/Z codes), even when they are not mental disorders, because they affect diagnosis, course, treatment, or safety.

## Examples
- Relational problems (parent-child, intimate partner, sibling)
- Abuse and neglect (child, partner, elder) and associated counseling
- Educational and occupational problems
- Housing and economic problems
- Problems related to crime, legal circumstances
- Other health service encounters (medication management)
- Nonadherence to medical treatment
- Bereavement (normal and complicated grief considerations)
- Religious or spiritual problems
- Acculturation and migration issues
- Lifestyle and health behavior problems
- Social exclusion or rejection; discrimination or persecution

## Clinical Use
- Document contextual factors impacting care
- Identify targets for intervention or referral
- Coordinate with social services, legal, educational, or community resources
