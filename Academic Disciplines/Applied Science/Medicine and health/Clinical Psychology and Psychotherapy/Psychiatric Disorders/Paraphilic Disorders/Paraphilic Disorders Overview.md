# Paraphilic Disorders Overview

Paraphilic disorders involve intense, persistent sexual interests that deviate from culturally normative consenting behaviors and cause distress/impairment or harm risk to others.

## Major Conditions (DSM-5)
- Voyeuristic, Exhibitionistic, Frotteuristic disorders
- Sexual masochism and sexual sadism disorders
- Pedophilic disorder
- Fetishistic disorder
- Transvestic disorder
- Other specified/unspecified paraphilic disorders

## Assessment
- Nature, duration, and intensity of interests
- Consent, legality, and risk assessment
- Distress and functional impact
- Comorbidities (impulse control, substance use)

## Etiology
- Conditioning and learning
- Cognitive distortions
- Impulse-control and neurobiological factors

## Treatment and Risk Management
- CBT targeting cognitive distortions and relapse prevention
- Behavioral interventions (covert sensitization, aversive techniques cautiously)
- Pharmacotherapy (antiandrogens, SSRIs in selected cases)
- Risk assessment and safeguarding

## Ethical and Legal Considerations
- Consent and harm prevention
- Mandatory reporting laws
