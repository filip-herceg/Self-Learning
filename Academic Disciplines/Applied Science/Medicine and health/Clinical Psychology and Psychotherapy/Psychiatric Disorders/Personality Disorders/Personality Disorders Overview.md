# Personality Disorders Overview

Personality disorders involve enduring, inflexible patterns of inner experience and behavior deviating from cultural expectations, leading to distress or impairment.

## DSM-5 Clusters
- Cluster A: Paranoid, Schizoid, Schizotypal
- Cluster B: Antisocial, Borderline, Histrionic, Narcissistic
- Cluster C: Avoidant, Dependent, Obsessive-Compulsive

## Assessment
- Structured clinical interviews (SCID-5-PD)
- Dimensional models (alternative model for PD)
- Longitudinal patterns and pervasiveness
- Risk (self-harm, impulsivity)

## Etiology
- Temperamental and genetic factors
- Attachment and early adversity
- Social learning and environment

## Treatment
- DBT (borderline), MBT, Schema Therapy, TFP
- CBT and social skills for avoidant/obsessive-compulsive PD
- Pharmacotherapy is adjunctive/symptom-targeted
- Therapeutic alliance and boundaries
