# Depressive Disorders Overview

Depressive disorders involve persistent low mood and related cognitive, behavioral, and somatic symptoms causing distress or impairment.

## Major Conditions
- Major depressive disorder (single/recurrent, specifiers)
- Persistent depressive disorder (dysthymia)
- Disruptive mood dysregulation disorder
- Premenstrual dysphoric disorder
- Other specified/unspecified depressive disorders

## Assessment
- Symptom severity and duration
- Suicidality and safety
- Functional impairment
- Medical contributors (thyroid, anemia, etc.)
- Rating scales (PHQ-9, HAM-D)

## Etiology
- Genetic and neurobiological factors
- Cognitive models (negative triad, rumination)
- Stress-diathesis and life events
- Inflammation and circadian rhythms

## Treatment
- CBT for depression; BA; IPT
- SSRIs/SNRIs; other antidepressants; augmentation strategies
- Exercise and lifestyle interventions
- Light therapy (seasonal)
- ECT, TMS, ketamine/esketamine for treatment-resistant cases

## Comorbidity
- Anxiety disorders
- Substance use
- Medical comorbidity
