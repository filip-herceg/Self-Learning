# Tic Disorders Overview

Tic disorders feature sudden, rapid, recurrent, nonrhythmic motor movements or vocalizations. Onset is typically in childhood.

## Major Conditions
- Touretteâ€™s disorder (multiple motor + at least one vocal tic, >1 year)
- Persistent (chronic) motor or vocal tic disorder
- Provisional tic disorder
- Other specified/unspecified tic disorders

## Assessment
- Onset, frequency, complexity, distribution
- Suppressibility and suggestibility
- Premonitory urges and relief after tics
- Functional impact (social, academic)
- Rule out chorea, seizures, stereotypies, substance/medication causes

## Etiology
- Genetic vulnerability and neurodevelopmental factors
- Cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical circuitry
- Stress and arousal effects

## Treatment
- Psychoeducation and monitoring
- Comprehensive Behavioral Intervention for Tics (CBIT/HRT)
- Exposure with response prevention for tics
- Alpha-2 agonists, antipsychotics for severe impairment
- Deep brain stimulation (rare, severe adult cases)

## Comorbidity
- ADHD
- OCD and related disorders
- Anxiety and mood disorders
