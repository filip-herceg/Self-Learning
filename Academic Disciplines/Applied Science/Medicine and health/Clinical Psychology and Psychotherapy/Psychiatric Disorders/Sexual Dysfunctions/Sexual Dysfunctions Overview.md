# Sexual Dysfunctions Overview

Sexual dysfunctions involve clinically significant disturbances in sexual desire, arousal, orgasm, or pain associated with sexual activity.

## Major Conditions
- Delayed ejaculation
- Erectile disorder
- Female orgasmic disorder
- Female sexual interest/arousal disorder
- Genito-pelvic pain/penetration disorder
- Male hypoactive sexual desire disorder
- Premature (early) ejaculation

## Assessment
- Onset (lifelong vs. acquired) and context (generalized vs. situational)
- Partner, relationship, and medical factors
- Medications/substances
- Cultural and religious considerations

## Etiology
- Biological factors (vascular, hormonal, neurological)
- Psychological factors (anxiety, beliefs, trauma)
- Relational factors

## Treatment
- Psychoeducation and sensate focus
- CBT and sex therapy techniques
- Pelvic floor therapy for pain disorders
- Pharmacotherapy (PDE5 inhibitors; off-label options)
- Couples therapy when indicated
