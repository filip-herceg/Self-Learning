# Psychiatric Disorders Overview

This section provides comprehensive coverage of major psychiatric and psychological disorders, their diagnostic criteria, etiology, and characteristics as defined in the DSM-5 and other diagnostic frameworks.

## Major Disorder Categories

- Neurodevelopmental Disorders — early-onset impairments in cognition, communication, learning, and motor function
- Tic Disorders — motor and vocal tic conditions (e.g., Tourette’s, chronic tic)
- Anxiety Disorders — excessive fear/anxiety with avoidance and physiological arousal
- Obsessive-Compulsive and Related Disorders — obsessions/compulsions and body-focused repetitive behaviors
- Trauma and Stressor-Related Disorders — sequelae of traumatic or stressful events (PTSD, acute stress, adjustment)
- Dissociative Disorders — disruptions in identity, memory, or consciousness
- Depressive Disorders — persistent low mood and associated cognitive/behavioral symptoms
- Bipolar and Related Disorders — mood episodes including mania/hypomania and depression
- Schizophrenia Spectrum and Other Psychotic Disorders — psychosis with delusions, hallucinations, disorganization, negative symptoms
- Personality Disorders — enduring, inflexible patterns causing distress/impairment
- Substance-Related and Addictive Disorders — problematic substance use and behavioral addictions
- Medication-Induced Movement Disorders and Other Adverse Effects — side effects of psychotropics and other meds
- Feeding and Eating Disorders — disturbed eating behaviors and body-related concerns
- Sleep-Wake Disorders — disturbances in sleep quantity, quality, timing, or behaviors during sleep
- Somatic Symptom and Related Disorders — excessive health concerns and somatic focus
- Disruptive, Impulse-Control, and Conduct Disorders — emotion/behavior control problems violating rights or norms
- Neurocognitive Disorders — acquired decline in cognitive domains
- Sexual Dysfunctions — disturbances in sexual desire, arousal, orgasm, or pain
- Gender Dysphoria — distress from incongruence between experienced gender and assigned sex
- Elimination Disorders — inappropriate elimination of urine or feces (typically childhood onset)
- Paraphilic Disorders — atypical sexual interests with distress/impairment or harm risk
- Other Conditions for Clinical Attention — V/Z codes for contextual factors affecting care
