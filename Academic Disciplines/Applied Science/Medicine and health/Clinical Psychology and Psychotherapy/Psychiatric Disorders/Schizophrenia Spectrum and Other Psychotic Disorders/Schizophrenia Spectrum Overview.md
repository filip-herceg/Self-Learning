# Schizophrenia Spectrum and Other Psychotic Disorders Overview

These disorders feature psychosis: delusions, hallucinations, disorganized thinking/behavior, and negative symptoms.

## Major Conditions
- Schizophrenia
- Schizoaffective disorder
- Schizophreniform disorder
- Brief psychotic disorder
- Delusional disorder
- Attenuated psychosis syndrome (research/other specified)

## Assessment
- Positive, negative, and cognitive symptoms
- Onset pattern and duration
- Substance/medical rule-outs
- Risk and safety (command hallucinations)
- Functional impairment

## Etiology
- Genetic vulnerability
- Neurodevelopmental factors
- Dopamine and glutamate hypotheses
- Stress and urbanicity

## Treatment
- Antipsychotic medications (first/second generation)
- Clozapine for treatment-resistant cases
- Psychoeducation and family intervention
- CBT for psychosis; social skills training
- Supported employment/education; cognitive remediation

## Course and Prognosis
- Early intervention (CHR services)
- Relapse prevention and adherence
- Addressing negative symptoms and cognition
