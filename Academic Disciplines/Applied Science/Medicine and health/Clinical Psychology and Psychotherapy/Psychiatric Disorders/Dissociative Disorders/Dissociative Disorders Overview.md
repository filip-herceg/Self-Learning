# Dissociative Disorders Overview

Dissociative disorders involve disruptions in consciousness, memory, identity, emotion, perception, or behavior.

## Major Conditions
- Dissociative identity disorder (DID)
- Dissociative amnesia (with/without fugue)
- Depersonalization/derealization disorder

## Assessment
- Dissociation screening tools (e.g., DES)
- Trauma history
- Rule out neurological and substance causes
- Safety and risk assessment

## Etiology
- Trauma and chronic stress
- Defensive dissociation mechanisms
- Developmental factors

## Treatment
- Phase-oriented trauma therapy (stabilization → processing → integration)
- Skills for grounding and emotion regulation
- Trauma-focused therapies (cautious pacing)
- Limited evidence for medications (symptom-targeted)

## Comorbidity
- PTSD and trauma-related disorders
- Mood and anxiety disorders
- Personality disorders
