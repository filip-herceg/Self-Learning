# Substance-Related and Addictive Disorders Overview

These disorders involve problematic use of substances or behaviors leading to clinically significant impairment or distress.

## Major Conditions
- Substance use disorders (alcohol, opioids, stimulants, sedatives, cannabis, hallucinogens, tobacco, inhalants)
- Gambling disorder
- Other (e.g., internet gaming disorder â€“ emerging)

## Assessment
- Use patterns and DSM criteria
- Withdrawal and tolerance
- Craving and loss of control
- Consequences and functional impact
- Readiness to change

## Etiology
- Genetic vulnerability and neuroadaptation
- Reward circuitry and dopamine
- Conditioning and cues
- Stress and environment

## Treatment
- Motivational interviewing and readiness work
- CBT/relapse prevention
- Contingency management
- Medications: naltrexone, acamprosate, buprenorphine/methadone, varenicline, disulfiram
- 12-step and mutual-help groups
- Harm reduction strategies
