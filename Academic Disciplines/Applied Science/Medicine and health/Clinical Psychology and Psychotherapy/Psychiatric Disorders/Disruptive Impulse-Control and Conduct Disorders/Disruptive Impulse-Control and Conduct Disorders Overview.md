# Disruptive, Impulse-Control, and Conduct Disorders Overview

These disorders involve problems in self-control of emotions and behaviors, violating rights of others or societal norms.

## Major Conditions
- Oppositional defiant disorder
- Intermittent explosive disorder
- Conduct disorder (childhood/adolescent onset)
- Pyromania
- Kleptomania

## Assessment
- Behavior patterns and contexts
- Severity, frequency, and harm
- Callous-unemotional traits
- Family, school, and legal impact

## Etiology
- Temperament and emotion regulation deficits
- Parenting practices and environment
- Peer influences
- Neurobiological factors (impulse control)

## Treatment
- Parent management training (PMT)
- CBT for anger and problem-solving
- Multisystemic therapy (MST) for conduct disorder
- School and community interventions
- Pharmacotherapy for comorbidities/impulsivity (when indicated)
