# Humanistic Therapies Overview

Humanistic therapies emphasize personal growth, self-actualization, and the client's subjective experience, operating from the belief in inherent human capacity for positive change and self-direction.

## Theoretical Foundations

- Person-centered theory (Carl Rogers)
- Existential philosophy and psychology
- Gestalt theory
- Existential-humanistic integration

## Core Approaches

### Person-Centered Therapy
- Unconditional positive regard
- Empathic understanding
- Congruence and authenticity
- Client-directed change

### Existential Therapy
- Freedom and responsibility
- Meaning and purpose
- Anxiety and authenticity
- Death awareness

### Gestalt Therapy
- Here-and-now awareness
- Contact and relationship
- Gestalt experiments and awareness
- Integration and wholeness

## Techniques and Interventions

- Active listening and reflection
- Exploration of experience
- Awareness exercises
- Experiential experiments
- Empty chair technique
- Authenticity and presence

## Applications

- Anxiety and depression
- Existential concerns
- Personal growth and development
- Creative and self-expressive issues
- End-of-life work
