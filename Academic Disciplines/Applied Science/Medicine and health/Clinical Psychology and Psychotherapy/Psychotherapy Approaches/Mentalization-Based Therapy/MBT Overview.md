# Mentalization-Based Therapy Overview

Mentalization-Based Therapy (MBT) focuses on enhancing mentalization—the capacity to understand mental states in oneself and others—particularly beneficial for personality disorders and attachment difficulties.

## Theoretical Foundations

- Mentalization theory
- Reflective function
- Attachment theory integration
- Neuroscience of mentalizing
- Psychodynamic framework

## Core Concepts

### Mentalization
- Ability to understand mental states
- Implicit and explicit mentalization
- Self-mentalizing and other-mentalizing
- Automatic and controlled processes

### Reflective Function
- Capacity to reflect on mental states
- Complexity and flexibility
- Development and variation
- Impairment in mental health conditions

### Domains of Mentalization
- Cognitive mentalization
- Affective mentalization
- Temporal mentalization
- Intentional vs. relational modes

## Therapeutic Approach

### Phases

**Initiation Phase**
- Building therapeutic alliance
- Assessment of mentalization capacity
- Relationship focus
- Safety and trust

**Development Phase**
- Enhancing mentalization
- Exploring mental states
- Reflective discussions
- Working with ruptures

**Consolidation and Discharge Phase**
- Integration of gains
- Maintenance strategies
- Independence
- Follow-up planning

### Core Techniques

- Mentalizing stance and curiosity
- Exploring thoughts and feelings
- Identifying mental states
- Reflecting on others' perspectives
- Addressing ruptures and difficulties
- Collaborative reflection

## Applications

### Primary: Personality Disorders
- Borderline personality disorder
- Antisensitivity personality organization
- Narcissistic personality
- Antisocial personality features

### Other Conditions
- Depression and anxiety
- Self-harm and suicidality
- Relationship difficulties
- Trauma and PTSD

### Special Contexts

**Individual Therapy**
- One-on-one treatment
- Intensive mentalization work
- Relationship focus

**Group Therapy**
- Mentalization in group context
- Peer learning and support
- Social learning

**Family Work**
- Family mentalization
- Parental mentalizing capacity
- Attachment patterns

- Therapist training
