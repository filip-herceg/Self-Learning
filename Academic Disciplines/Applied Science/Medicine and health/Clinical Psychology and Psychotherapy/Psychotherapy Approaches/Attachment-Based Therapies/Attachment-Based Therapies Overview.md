# Attachment-Based Therapies Overview

Attachment-based therapies utilize attachment theory to understand and treat relational problems, focusing on the therapeutic relationship as a corrective emotional experience and secure base for growth.

## Theoretical Foundations

- Attachment theory (Bowlby, Ainsworth)
- Internal working models
- Secure base and safe haven
- Earned secure attachment
- Relational neuroscience

## Core Concepts

- Attachment styles and patterns
- Secure and insecure attachment
- Intergenerational transmission
- Relational patterns
- Trust and safety

## Major Attachment-Based Approaches

### Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT)
- Attachment-focused couples therapy
- Demand-withdraw patterns
- Emotional accessibility and responsiveness
- Bonding and connection
- Systemic and experiential focus

### Emotion-Focused Individual Therapy
- Personal vulnerability
- Emotional experiencing
- Attachment needs
- Self-compassion and care

### Parent-Child Interaction Therapy (PCIT)
- Attachment-focused parenting
- Parent coaching
- Child behavioral change
- Relationship strengthening

### Dyadic Developmental Psychotherapy (DDP)
- Attachment-based parenting
- Trauma and adoption focus
- Playful engagement
- PACE principles (Playfulness, Acceptance, Curiosity, Empathy)

### Internal Family Systems (IFS) Therapy
- Parts and internal relationships
- Self-to-part attachment
- Self leadership
- Healing inner attachments

## Therapeutic Processes

- Safety and secure base creation
- Emotional processing
- Repair and responsiveness
- Vulnerability and authenticity
- Corrective experiences

## Techniques

- Emotional validation and attunement
- Attachment exploration
- Pattern identification
- Restructuring interactions
- Increasing accessibility
- Enactment and experiential work

## Applications

### Couple and Relationship Therapy
- Relationship conflicts
- Intimacy and connection
- Infidelity and betrayal
- Separation and divorce

### Family Therapy
- Parent-child conflicts
- Parenting challenges
- Intergenerational patterns
- Adoption and foster care

### Individual Psychotherapy
- Attachment difficulties
- Relationship patterns
- Trauma and abuse
- Anxiety and depression
- Personality disorders

### Special Populations
- Children and adolescents
- Adoptees and foster children
- Trauma survivors
- Attachment disorders

## Integration with Other Approaches

- Trauma-informed practice
- Somatic approaches
- Mindfulness and compassion
- Cognitive-behavioral integration

## Research and Evidence
