# Cognitive Behavioral Therapy Overview

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is an evidence-based psychotherapy focusing on the relationships among thoughts, feelings, and behaviors, aiming to modify maladaptive thinking patterns and behaviors.

## Core Concepts

- Cognitive model: thoughts → feelings → behaviors
- Automatic thoughts and cognitive distortions
- Beliefs and schemas
- Behavioral activation and experiential learning

## Applications

### Anxiety Disorders
- Generalized anxiety disorder
- Panic disorder and agoraphobia
- Social anxiety disorder
- Specific phobias

### Mood Disorders
- Major depression
- Bipolar disorder (adjunctive)

### Trauma and PTSD
- Trauma-focused CBT
- Prolonged exposure therapy
- Cognitive processing therapy

### Other Disorders
- OCD and related disorders
- Eating disorders
- Substance use disorders
- Insomnia and sleep disorders

## Techniques and Interventions

- Cognitive restructuring
- Behavioral experiments
- Thought records
- Behavioral activation
- Exposure and response prevention
- Skills training

## Research and Evidence

- Empirical support across disorders
- Mechanisms of change
- Efficacy and effectiveness
- Adaptations and cultural considerations
