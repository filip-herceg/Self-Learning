# Dialectical Behavior Therapy Overview

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) is an evidence-based psychotherapy combining cognitive-behavioral techniques with dialectical philosophy and mindfulness, particularly effective for emotion dysregulation.

## Theoretical Foundations

- Dialectical philosophy (thesis-antithesis-synthesis)
- Biosocial theory
- Dialectics of validation and change
- Cognitive-behavioral framework
- Acceptance and commitment principles

## Core Concepts

- Dialectics in therapy
- Validation and change
- Emotion dysregulation
- Distress tolerance
- Mindfulness and acceptance

## Key Components

### Skills Training Modules

**Mindfulness**
- Present moment awareness
- Non-judgmental observation
- Participation

**Distress Tolerance**
- Crisis survival strategies
- Self-soothing techniques
- Distracting and self-respect
- TIPP skills (Temperature, Intense exercise, Paced breathing, Paired muscle relaxation)

**Emotion Regulation**
- Identifying and understanding emotions
- Reducing vulnerability
- ABC PLEASE skills
- Opposite action
- Checking the facts

**Interpersonal Effectiveness**
- Assertiveness and communication
- Saying no
- Asking for needs
- Managing relationships
- DEAR MAN, GIVE, FAST skills

### Individual Therapy

- Problem solving

- Improving motivation

- Skills support between sessions
- Crisis coaching
- Brief interventions

### Therapist Consultation Team
- Case consultation
- Therapist support

## Applications

### Primary Application: Borderline Personality Disorder
- Emotional dysregulation
- Self-harm behaviors
- Suicidality
- Relationship instability

### Other Disorders and Populations
- Bipolar disorder
- PTSD and trauma
- Eating disorders
- Substance use disorders
- Self-harm and deliberate injury
- Adolescents with emotional dysregulation
