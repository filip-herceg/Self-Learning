# Solution-Focused Brief Therapy Overview

Solution-Focused Brief Therapy (SFBT) is a goal-directed, collaborative approach emphasizing client strengths, resources, and preferred futures, with focus on solutions rather than problems.

## Theoretical Foundations

- Social constructionism
- Narrative approaches
- Resource and resilience focus
- Collaborative relationship
- Pragmatism and efficiency

## Core Principles

- Assume clients have strengths and resources
- Focus on solutions and goals
- Small changes lead to larger changes
- Unique solutions exist
- Language shapes reality
- Respect client expertise

## Key Concepts

### Problem-Saturated Narratives
- Identifying dominant problem stories
- Externalizing problems
- Alternative stories

### Preferred Futures
- Vision and goals
- Values and meaning
- Possibility thinking

### Resources and Strengths
- Identifying existing strengths
- Hidden resources and abilities
- Resilience factors

### Exceptions and Unique Solutions
- When problems don't happen
- Previous successes
- Building on existing solutions

## Main Techniques

### Miracle Question
- Envisioning a future without the problem
- Detailed description of changes
- Implications and meaning

### Scaling Questions
- Quantifying progress and goals
- Movement tracking
- Motivation and hope

### Exception Finding
- When problems are less severe
- Identifying patterns
- Building on exceptions

### Compliments and Acknowledgment
- Recognizing strengths and efforts
- Motivation and engagement
- Positive reinforcement

### Goal Setting
- Clear and achievable goals
- Measurable progress indicators
- Client-defined success

### Coping Questions
- Existing strategies
- Resilience demonstration
- Resource activation

## Applications

### Mental Health Issues
- Depression and anxiety
- Relationship problems
- Family conflicts
- Behavioral issues
- Grief and loss

### Life Transitions
- Career changes
- Educational challenges
- Parenting issues
- Adjustment to change

### Organizational and School Settings
- Classroom management
- Organizational development
- Conflict resolution
- Performance improvement

## Brief Format

- Typically 1-5 sessions
- Focused and goal-directed
- Efficiency and accessibility
- Prevention and early intervention

## Evidence and Research

- Efficacy for various presenting problems
- Brief therapy outcomes
