# Psychodynamic Therapy Overview

Psychodynamic psychotherapy is based on psychoanalytic theory, emphasizing unconscious processes, early relationships, and insight into internal conflicts as mechanisms of psychological change.

## Historical Development

- Freudian psychoanalysis foundations
- Neo-Freudian perspectives
- Object relations theory
- Attachment theory influences
- Modern psychodynamic integration

## Core Concepts

- Unconscious processes and defense mechanisms
- Internal conflicts and ambivalence
- Transference and countertransference
- Insight and interpretation
- Symbolic meaning and associations

## Therapeutic Techniques

- Free association
- Dream analysis
- Interpretation of defenses
- Analysis of transference
- Working through conflicts

## Theoretical Models

- Classical psychoanalytic theory
- Object relations approaches
- Interpersonal psychoanalysis
- Relational psychoanalysis

## Applications and Adaptations

- Short-term dynamic therapy
- Mentalization-based therapy
- Schema therapy
- Transference-focused psychotherapy

## Evidence Base

- Research on mechanisms
- Efficacy for various disorders
- Long-term outcomes
- Considerations for modern practice
