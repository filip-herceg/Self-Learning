# Motivational Interviewing Overview

Motivational Interviewing (MI) is a client-centered, evidence-based approach facilitating behavior change through evoking and strengthening intrinsic motivation and commitment to change.

## Theoretical Foundations

- Stages of change model
- Self-determination theory
- Ambivalence and resistance
- Intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation
- Collaborative partnership

## Core Spirit of MI

- Partnership
- Evocation (drawing out motivation)
- Compassion
- Autonomy support

## Key Principles

- Express empathy
- Develop discrepancy
- Roll with resistance
- Support self-efficacy

## Stages of Change

### Precontemplation
- Not considering change
- Increasing awareness

### Contemplation
- Weighing pros and cons
- Ambivalence and decision-making

### Preparation
- Commitment to change
- Planning action

### Action
- Active behavior change
- Following through on plans

### Maintenance
- Sustaining new behaviors
- Preventing relapse

### Relapse
- Return to old behaviors
- Recycling through stages

## Core Techniques

### Open-Ended Questions
- Exploration and elaboration
- Client narrative
- Motivation and values

### Affirmations
- Recognition of strengths
- Resilience and capability
- Engagement and hope

### Reflective Listening
- Simple reflections
- Complex reflections
- Amplifying and shifting

### Summarization
- Gathering information
- Clarifying ambivalence
- Moving toward commitment

### Evoking Change Talk
- Mobilizing motivation
- Strengthening commitment
- Planning and action

## Applications

### Substance Use Disorders
- Alcohol dependence and abuse
- Drug addiction
- Smoking cessation
- Gambling disorders

### Health Behaviors
- Medication adherence
- Exercise and physical activity
- Diet and nutrition
- Sexual health practices
- Medical compliance

### Mental Health Conditions
- Depression treatment engagement
- Anxiety management
- Eating disorders
- Dual diagnosis

### Special Populations
- Adolescents and young adults
- Criminal justice system
- Healthcare settings
- Occupational health

## Variants and Adaptations

- Brief interventions
- Group MI
- Telephone-based MI
- Digital and online MI

## Research and Evidence

- Efficacy for behavior change
- Mechanisms of change
- Comparison with other approaches
- Implementation and fidelity
