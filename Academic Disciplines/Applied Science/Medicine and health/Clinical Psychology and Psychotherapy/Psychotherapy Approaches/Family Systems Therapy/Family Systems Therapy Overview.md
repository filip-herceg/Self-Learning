# Family Systems Therapy Overview

Family systems therapy views families as interconnected systems and emphasizes understanding individual problems within relational and systemic context, focusing on interactions and dynamics.

## Theoretical Foundations

- General systems theory
- Cybernetics and feedback loops
- Multigenerational patterns
- Family structures and hierarchies

## Major Approaches

### Structural Family Therapy
- Family structures and subsystems
- Boundaries and hierarchy
- Joining and accommodation
- Restructuring interventions

### Systemic/Strategic Family Therapy
- Circular causality
- Feedback loops and patterns
- Reframing
- Prescriptive interventions

### Bowen Family Systems Theory
- Differentiation of self
- Triangulation
- Multigenerational transmission
- Genograms and family patterns

### Narrative Family Therapy
- Problem-saturated narratives
- Alternative stories
- Externalization
- Collaborative approach

## Techniques and Interventions

- Structural mapping and reframing
- Circular questioning
- Genogram construction
- Behavioral prescriptions
- Therapeutic conversations
- Relabeling and externalization

## Applications

- Family conflict
- Individual problems with family involvement
- Couple relationships
- Parenting and child behavior
- Intergenerational issues
- Divorce and blended families

## Research and Evidence

- Research on family processes
- Efficacy for various presenting problems
- Cultural considerations
- Integration with individual therapy
