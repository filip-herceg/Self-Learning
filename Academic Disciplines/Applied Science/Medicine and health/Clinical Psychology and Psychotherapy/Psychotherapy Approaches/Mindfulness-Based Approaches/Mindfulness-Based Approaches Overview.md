# Mindfulness-Based Approaches Overview

Mindfulness-based approaches integrate formal meditation and informal mindfulness practice with therapeutic interventions to reduce suffering, enhance awareness, and promote psychological well-being.

## Core Principles

- Present-moment awareness
- Non-judgmental observation
- Acceptance and allowing
- Decentering from thoughts
- Compassion and kindness

## Major Protocols

### Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR)
- Mindfulness meditation
- Body scan
- Yoga and movement
- Everyday mindfulness practice

### Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT)
- Combination of MBSR and CBT
- Relapse prevention focus
- Cognitive patterns and rumination
- Development of decentering

### Acceptance and Mindfulness-Based Approaches
- Dialectical Behavior Therapy (see separate entry)
- Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (see separate entry)

### Mindful Self-Compassion (MSC)
- Loving-kindness meditation
- Compassionate responding
- Self-kindness practices
- Shared humanity

### Other Mindfulness Therapies
- Compassion-Focused Therapy
- Mindfulness-Based Relapse Prevention (MBRP)
- Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy for Psychosis (MBCT-P)

## Mechanisms of Change

- Attention and metacognition
- Emotion regulation
- Acceptance and willingness
- Decentering and perspective
- Neuroplasticity and brain changes

## Techniques and Practices

### Formal Meditation
- Mindfulness meditation
- Body scan
- Loving-kindness meditation
- Walking meditation
- Labeling and noting practices

### Informal Mindfulness
- Everyday mindfulness
- Mindful eating, walking, listening
- Awareness in daily activities
- Informal practice integration

### Yoga and Movement
- Mindful yoga
- Movement meditation
- Embodied awareness
