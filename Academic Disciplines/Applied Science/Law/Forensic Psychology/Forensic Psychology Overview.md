# Forensic Psychology Overview

Forensic psychology applies psychological principles and practices to the legal system, including criminal investigation, trial testimony, offender assessment, and victim support.

## Core Areas

### Criminal Justice Applications
- Criminal profiling and behavioral analysis
- Interviewing and interrogation
- Lie detection and polygraph examination
- Eyewitness memory and testimony
- Victim and offender assessment

### Civil and Family Law
- Child custody evaluations
- Competency assessments
- Guardianship and conservatorship
- Intimate partner violence assessment
- Child maltreatment assessment

### Trial Consultation and Expert Testimony
- Expert witness role and testimony
- Jury selection and behavior
- Trial preparation
- Ethical issues in testimony

### Offender Assessment and Treatment
- Risk assessment and prediction
- Classification and profiling
- Rehabilitation programs
- Recidivism and monitoring

### Mental Health and the Legal System
- Competency to stand trial
- Insanity and fitness to plead
- Diminished capacity
- Commitment and involuntary hospitalization

### Victim Services
- Trauma assessment
- Crisis intervention
- Support and advocacy
- Compensation issues

## Research Areas

- Eyewitness reliability
- False confessions
- Risk assessment instruments
- Treatment efficacy
- Evidence-based practices

## Ethical and Legal Issues

- Confidentiality and privilege
- Conflicts of interest
- Standards of practice
- Admissibility of psychological evidence
