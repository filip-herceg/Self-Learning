# Quantitative Psychology Overview

Quantitative psychology develops and applies mathematical and statistical methods for psychological research and assessment, including psychometrics, statistical modeling, and research methodology.

## Core Topics

### Measurement and Psychometrics
- Test construction and validation
- Reliability and validity
- Item response theory (IRT)
- Classical test theory
- Scale development
- Bias in measurement
- Fairness in testing

### Statistical Methods
- Descriptive and inferential statistics
- Hypothesis testing
- Effect sizes and power analysis
- Bayesian methods
- Meta-analysis

### Multivariate Statistics
- Multiple regression and prediction
- Factor analysis and dimensionality reduction
- Structural equation modeling (SEM)
- Cluster analysis
- Discriminant analysis

### Advanced Methods
- Multilevel modeling
- Time series analysis
- Growth curve modeling
- Network analysis
- Computational modeling

### Research Design
- Experimental design
- Quasi-experimental designs
- Survey design and sampling
- Longitudinal designs
- Mixed methods research

### Assessment and Evaluation
- Program evaluation
- Educational assessment
- Clinical assessment development
- Occupational assessment

### Data Analysis and Visualization
- Data management and cleaning
- Missing data handling
- Data visualization
- Statistical software and coding
- Reproducible research

### Methodological Issues
- Statistical assumptions and violations
- Type I and Type II errors
- Publication bias and replication
- Open science practices
- Ethical considerations in research

## Applications

- Test development and validation
- Psychological research
- Organizational assessment
- Clinical and educational evaluation
