# Psychology Overview

Psychology is the scientific study of behavior and mental processes. This section covers core subdisciplines of academic psychology that investigate normal psychological processes across different domains.

## Core Subdisciplines

### Fundamental Domains
- **Behavioral Psychology**: Learning mechanisms, conditioning, and reinforcement
- **Cognitive Psychology**: Mental processes including perception, memory, thinking, and problem-solving
- **Developmental Psychology**: Psychological development across the lifespan
- **Personality Psychology**: Individual differences, traits, and personality theories
- **Social Psychology**: Social behavior, attitudes, and group dynamics
- **Abnormal Psychology**: Psychological disorders and maladaptive behavior patterns
- **Health Psychology**: Psychological factors in physical health and illness

### Specialized and Applied Domains
- **Industrial-Organizational Psychology**: Workplace behavior, performance, and organizational dynamics
- **Environmental Psychology**: Person-environment interactions and environmental effects
- **Community Psychology**: Prevention, empowerment, and social justice in communities
- **Cross-Cultural Psychology**: Cultural influences on behavior and cognition
- **Evolutionary Psychology**: Adaptive functions and evolutionary origins of behavior
- **Comparative Psychology**: Behavior across animal species and species comparisons
- **Quantitative Psychology**: Statistical methods, psychometrics, and research design
- **Political Psychology**: Political behavior, attitudes, and group dynamics
- **Consumer Psychology**: Consumer behavior, decision-making, and marketing
- **Occupational Health Psychology**: Worker well-being, safety, and occupational stress

## Applied Fields

For practical applications of psychology, see:
- **Clinical Psychology and Psychotherapy** (Applied Science) - Assessment, diagnosis, and treatment
- **Educational Psychology** (Applied Science) - Learning and education
- **Forensic Psychology** (Applied Science) - Legal and criminal justice
- **Sports Psychology** (Applied Science) - Athletic performance and mental health

## Related Disciplines

- **Neuroscience** (Natural Science) - Biological bases of behavior
- **Philosophy of Mind** (Humanities) - Theoretical foundations
