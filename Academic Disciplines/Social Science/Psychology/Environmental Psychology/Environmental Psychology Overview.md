# Environmental Psychology Overview

Environmental psychology examines the relationships between people and their physical and natural environments, investigating how environmental factors influence behavior, cognition, and well-being.

## Core Topics

### Environmental Perception and Cognition
- Environmental perception and sensory experience
- Spatial cognition and wayfinding
- Environmental aesthetics and preference
- Cognitive maps and mental representations

### Human Responses to Environment
- Stress and restoration
- Environmental stressors (noise, crowding, pollution)
- Restorative environments and attention recovery
- Biophilia and nature connection

### Environmental Behavior
- Pro-environmental behavior and conservation
- Sustainability and consumer choices
- Resource use and waste
- Energy consumption and conservation

### Design and Built Environment
- Architectural psychology
- Urban design and livability
- Home environment
- Workplace design
- Educational facility design
- Healthcare facility design

### Natural Environment
- Human relationship with nature
- Outdoor recreation
- Wilderness experience
- Environmental attachment and place identity

### Environmental Stressors and Health
- Air, water, and light pollution
- Noise and acoustics
- Temperature and climate
- Disaster and emergency psychology

### Climate and Environmental Change
- Psychological aspects of climate change
- Climate anxiety and eco-anxiety
- Adaptation to environmental change
- Social dimensions of sustainability

## Research Methods

- Environmental observation
- Simulation and virtual environments
- Behavioral mapping
- Survey and interview methods
