# Developmental Psychology Overview

Developmental psychology studies how people change and grow throughout the lifespan, examining physical, cognitive, emotional, and social development from infancy to old age.

## Topics

### Infancy and Childhood
- Prenatal development
- Neonatal period and infancy
- Cognitive development (Piaget, information processing)
- Language development
- Attachment and socialization
- Play and peer relationships

### Adolescence
- Biological changes and puberty
- Identity formation
- Cognitive development and abstract thinking
- Social relationships and peer influence
- Risk-taking and decision-making

### Early and Middle Adulthood
- Cognitive development in adulthood
- Career development
- Intimate relationships and attachment
- Parenting
- Life transitions

### Late Adulthood
- Cognitive aging
- Memory and aging
- Personality and adjustment
- Wisdom and meaning-making
- Death and dying

### Theoretical Perspectives
- Psychoanalytic theories (Freud, Erikson)
- Cognitive-developmental theories
- Learning theories
- Ecological systems theory
- Attachment theory
