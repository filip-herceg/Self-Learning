# Occupational Health Psychology Overview

Occupational health psychology applies psychological principles to create safe, healthy, and productive work environments, addressing workplace stressors and promoting worker well-being.

## Core Topics

### Workplace Stress and Strain
- Job stress models and theories
- Work-related stressors
- Role stress and conflict
- Job demands and resources
- Burnout and exhaustion
- Compassion fatigue

### Physical Health at Work
- Occupational hazards and injuries
- Ergonomics and biomechanics
- Illness prevention programs
- Health promotion in workplace
- Disability and return-to-work

### Psychological Factors in Health
- Psychosomatic disorders
- Stress and immune function
- Sleep and shift work effects
- Substance abuse in workplace
- Mental health and work

### Job Performance and Safety
- Safety culture and behavior
- Accident prevention
- Hazard perception
- Risk assessment and management
- Error and incident analysis

### Organizational Factors
- Organizational culture and climate
- Leadership and safety
- Communication and coordination
- Organizational change and stress
- Workload and scheduling

### Psychosocial Hazards
- Interpersonal conflict and aggression
- Bullying and harassment
- Violence in workplace
- Discrimination and inequity
- Psychological contracts

### Special Work Populations
- High-stress occupations (emergency responders, healthcare)
- Remote and flexible work
- Shift workers and unusual schedules
- Individuals with disabilities
- Aging workforce

### Well-Being Initiatives
- Stress management programs
- Employee assistance programs (EAP)
- Wellness and prevention programs
- Work-life balance initiatives
- Positive organizational practices

### Rehabilitation and Return-to-Work
- Injury rehabilitation
- Disability management
- Modified duty and accommodations
- Vocational rehabilitation
- Therapeutic work
