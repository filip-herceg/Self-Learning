# Consumer Psychology Overview

Consumer psychology investigates psychological processes involved in consumer decision-making, purchase behavior, and interactions with marketing and advertising.

## Core Topics

### Consumer Decision-Making
- Problem recognition and needs
- Information search and processing
- Evaluation of alternatives
- Purchase decisions
- Post-purchase behavior and satisfaction
- Consumer loyalty and repeat purchase

### Consumer Motivation
- Motivation and goals
- Hedonic vs. utilitarian consumption
- Emotional motivation
- Self-concept and identity
- Status and prestige motivation

### Attitudes and Beliefs
- Attitude formation toward products/brands
- Brand attitudes and preferences
- Belief structures
- Attitude change and resistance
- Brand loyalty and commitment

### Influence and Persuasion
- Advertising effectiveness
- Celebrity endorsements
- Social proof and conformity
- Authority and credibility
- Message framing and appeals

### Learning and Memory
- Classical conditioning in marketing
- Learning and brand associations
- Memory for advertisements
- Brand recall and recognition

### Individual Differences
- Personality and consumer behavior
- Lifestyle and psychographics
- Risk perception and tolerance
- Impulsivity and self-control
- Cultural and demographic factors

### Digital and Online Consumer Behavior
- E-commerce and online shopping
- Social media and consumer behavior
- Online reviews and reputation
- Digital marketing and targeting
- Privacy concerns and trust

### Consumer Well-Being and Ethics
- Materialism and well-being
- Sustainable consumption
- Ethical concerns in marketing
- Consumer protection and regulation
- Addiction and compulsive buying

### Specialized Consumer Segments
- Adolescent and youth consumers
- Elderly consumers
- Low-income consumers
- Cultural and ethnic consumer groups

## Applications

- Market research
- Advertising strategy
- Brand management
- Consumer policy
