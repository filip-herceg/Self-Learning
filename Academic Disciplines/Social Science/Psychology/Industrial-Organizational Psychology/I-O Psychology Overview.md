# Industrial-Organizational Psychology Overview

Industrial-Organizational (I-O) Psychology applies psychological principles to workplace settings, examining employee behavior, organizational dynamics, and factors affecting job performance and satisfaction.

## Core Topics

### Personnel Selection and Assessment
- Job analysis and competency modeling
- Recruitment and hiring practices
- Employee selection instruments
- Structured interviews
- Psychological testing in workplace

### Performance Management
- Performance appraisal systems
- Goal-setting and feedback
- Motivation and incentives
- Performance improvement interventions

### Training and Development
- Training needs assessment
- Instructional design
- Skill development programs
- Leadership development
- Organizational learning

### Organizational Behavior
- Individual differences and work
- Motivation theories in workplace
- Group dynamics and team performance
- Organizational culture and climate
- Change management

### Work and Wellbeing
- Job satisfaction and engagement
- Burnout and stress
- Work-life balance
- Occupational health and safety
- Organizational justice

### Organizational Development
- Organizational design
- Systems change
- Consultation and intervention
- Organizational effectiveness

### Leadership and Management
- Leadership theories and styles
- Transformational leadership
- Team leadership
- Emotional intelligence
- Executive coaching

## Research Methods

- Organizational surveys
- Longitudinal studies
- Intervention research
- Program evaluation
